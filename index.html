Week 1

Introductions

Materials

  • Laptop + Charger
  • Masks + Latex Gloves
  • Notebook
  • Pens/ Pencils
  • Elegoo Uno Super Kit

Tin Foil Boats

Dive into engineering and the engineering design process by building tin foil boats that can hold a heavy load of coins. We will discuss both the success and challenges of their first prototype before students get to test and build a second version of their tin foil boat.

Battery Powered LEDs

Our first adventure into electronics - no computer necessary. We will be learning the basics of building circuits using a 9V battery, some LEDs (light emitting diodes) and a breadboard. Some questions we will answer: How do breadboards work? Why are some of the lights brighter than others? What's the importance of the positive and negative in a circuit?

Secret Messages: Morse Code

Now that we can light LEDS up, can we use them to send secret messages? After experimenting with just using the battery and LEDs to send messages, we'll bring the Arduino and a button into the circuit to improve our secret message sending.

Week 3 + week 4

Social Distancing Headbands

Materials

  • Laptop + Charger
  • Masks + Latex Gloves
  • Notebook
  • Pens/ Pencils
  • Elegoo Uno Super Kit
  • (optional) Extra Supplies for Headband Prototype

Paper Plane Targets

A quick warm-up activity, students will have 15 minutes to make paper airplanes out of 2 pieces of paper. There will be a target to aim for and students will see who can make the farthest flying + most accurate plane.

Headband Prototype

Students will be given cardboard, tape, paper, fabric, beads, string and paint to build the prototype of their socially distancing headband. After a prototype is built, there will be a walkthrough on how to wire and attach the ultrasonic sensor

3D Printing Basics

If there is time, students will be introduced to 3D Modeling using Tinkercad and can begin to create their custom chess pieces for the first 3D printing project.
