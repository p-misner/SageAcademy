Necessary Electronics

To complete the project, each student will use:

  • 1 Piezo Speaker
  • 1 Arduino
  • 1 Breadboard
  • 1 9V battery and 1 9V battery to Arduino connector
  • Minimum of three 2kΩ ressitors
  • Wires
Single Fruit Circuit
Breadboard Connections

1. Connect 5V pin on the Arduino to the long vertical red + row on your Breadboard

2. Connect GND pin on the Arduino to the long vertical blue - row on your Breadboard

Piezo Speaker Connections

1. Connect one pin on the Piezo Speaker to the 3 pin on the Arduino

2. Place the other pin on the Piezo Speaker in the long vertical - row in your Breadboard (ground).

Fruit Connections

1. Connect the A5 pin on the Arduino to on e of the short horizontal rows in the Breadboard

2. In the same horizontal row as before, use the 2kΩ resistor to connect the horizontal row to the long vertical + row (red) in the Breadboard

3. In the same horizontal row as before, place one end of the wire into the row and the other into a piece of fruit.

Tin Foil

1. Place a black wire in the long vertical - row in your Breadboard (ground). Do not connect it to anything

2. Wrap tin foil around the metal edge of the black wire

3. Tape the tinfoil (connected to the blackwire) to the table.

Complete Circuit Picture
Multi-Fruit Circuit

This circuit is very similar to the Single Fruit circuit except for the fact you connect more fruit to more analog pins

Fruit Connections

1. Connect the [A4, A3, A2, A1, A0] pin on the Arduino to one of the short horizontal rows in the Breadboard

2. In the same horizontal row as before, use the 2kΩ resistor to connect the horizontal row to the long vertical + row (red) in the Breadboard

3. In the same horizontal row as before, place one end of the wire into the row and the other into a piece of fruit.

Code

Single Fruit + Single Note

Make sure the fruit wire is plugged into pin A5 on the Arduino


#define TONE_USE_INT
#define TONE_PITCH 440
#include 

const int buzzer = 3;

void setup() {
  pinMode(buzzer, OUTPUT);
  Serial.begin(9600)  ;
}

void loop() {
   if (analogRead(A5) < 1022){
     Serial.println("A");
     tone(buzzer,NOTE_A4);    
   } 
   else {

    noTone(buzzer);
   }
   delay(200);
}
