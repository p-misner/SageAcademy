Cookie Cutter

To make a cookie cutter in Tinkercad, students will first sketch the design they want before emailing it to Priya. Priya will email you back a .svg file. Using that file, follow the steps below.

Importing SVG
Step 1

Go to Tinkercad and enter your first name all lowercase as your username

Step 2

Once you join, select '3D Designs' from the menu on the right and then click the box containig the design you want to edit or the button titled 'Create new design'

Step 3

Do you rememember the file name from the email Priya sent you? If not, go to your email and write down the file name. It should be something like "name.svg". Click the import button shown with the blue arrow.

Step 4

Click the "choose file" button show in blue and selec the filename you've written down in Step 3 from the dropdown.

Step 5

After selecting a file, you should see the screen in the picture below. Click the blue import button that has the blue arrows pointing to it.

Step 6

All done! Your screen should look like this. Fiddle with the model until you are happy with it

Exporting STL
Step 1

Go to Tinkercad and enter your first name all lowercase as your username

Step 2

Once you join, select '3D Designs' from the menu on the right and then click the box containig the design you want to edit or the button titled 'Create new design'

Step 3

When you are done modeling, click the object you want to 3D print then click the export button, shown with the blue arrow.

Step 4

After clicking the export button, the screen in the image below pops up. Click on the STL option circled in blue. This downloads the file you need to send to Priya.

Step 5

Either email the downloaded file to Priya or submit it on schoology through the 'Personal 3D Printed Objects' assignment
